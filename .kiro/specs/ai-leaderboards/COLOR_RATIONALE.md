# AI Leaderboards Color Rationale

## Overview

The color scheme for the AI Leaderboards rank system is carefully designed to create a clear visual hierarchy, convey progression, and leverage color psychology to enhance player motivation and engagement.

## Design Principles

### 1. Progressive Intensity
Colors increase in vibrancy and intensity as ranks get higher, creating a visual sense of progression and achievement.

### 2. Color Psychology
Each color is chosen based on psychological associations that align with the rank's prestige level.

### 3. Accessibility
All colors meet WCAG AA contrast standards and are distinguishable for users with color vision deficiencies.

### 4. Cultural Universality
Colors are chosen to have positive associations across different cultures.

## Rank Color Breakdown

## Tier 1: Entry Ranks (0-19 wins)

### Beginner - Gray (#9CA3AF)

**Color Choice**: Neutral gray

**Psychology**:
- **Neutral**: Represents a blank slate, starting point
- **Humble**: No pretense, everyone starts here
- **Potential**: Gray is the foundation before color emerges

**Design Rationale**:
- Doesn't intimidate new players
- Clearly indicates "starting rank"
- Provides contrast for progression to colored ranks
- Universal and non-threatening

**Cultural Associations**:
- Western: Neutrality, balance, beginning
- Eastern: Simplicity, modesty, foundation

---

### Novice - Light Gray (#D1D5DB)

**Color Choice**: Lighter gray (almost silver)

**Psychology**:
- **Progress**: Slightly brighter than Beginner
- **Learning**: Still in training phase
- **Hope**: Beginning to see the light

**Design Rationale**:
- Subtle progression from Beginner
- Hints at the "silver" of higher ranks
- Still humble but showing improvement
- Easy visual distinction from Beginner

**Cultural Associations**:
- Western: Silver lining, potential
- Eastern: Purity, new beginnings

---

### Fighter - Blue (#60A5FA)

**Color Choice**: Bright blue

**Psychology**:
- **Confidence**: First colored rank shows competence
- **Trust**: Blue is universally trusted

- **Stability**: Solid foundation established

**Design Rationale**:
- First "real" color marks important milestone
- Blue is calming yet confident
- Signals transition from beginner to intermediate
- Creates excitement for progression

**Cultural Associations**:
- Western: Trust, confidence, stability
- Eastern: Immortality, advancement, healing

## Accessibility Considerations

### Color Blindness

**Protanopia (Red-Blind)**:
- Blue, green, purple remain distinct
- Gold appears yellow (still distinct)
- Red appears brown/yellow (distinct from others)

**Deuteranopia (Green-Blind)**:
- Blue, purple, gold, red remain distinct
- Green appears yellow/brown (distinct from blue)

**Tritanopia (Blue-Blind)**:
- All colors remain reasonably distinct
- Blue appears green (but context helps)

**Solution**: Rank names and icons provide additional identification beyond color alone.
